Section 03 · Investment Process

How an idea
becomes a position.

Every stock in the Janes portfolio has been through a five-stage process. The process is what separates conviction from enthusiasm.

01 — Workflow

Five stages, left to right.

Natural mapping
01
Screen
Quantitative and qualitative screens narrow the universe to candidates.
02
Diligence
Financial modeling, management research, channel checks, primary sources.
03
Pitch
Formal presentation to the fund. Thesis, catalysts, valuation, risk, sizing.
04
Vote
Members vote. A position requires conviction beyond a simple majority.
05
Own
The analyst maintains the position. Quarterly updates. Sell discipline enforced.
02 — Checklist

What every pitch must answer.

01 · Thesis
Why this, why now?
A single sentence anyone can repeat. The "now" matters as much as the "why" — without a catalyst, conviction decays into hope.
02 · Catalysts
What forces re-rating?
Earnings, regulatory, structural. The pitch must name 2–3 specific events on a 6–18 month horizon that close the gap.
03 · Risk
What could we be wrong about?
Pre-mortem before purchase. If the position is wrong, what's the signal that confirms it? What's the stop-out?
04 · Sizing
How much, and how scaled?
Position size reflects conviction, asymmetry, and correlation with existing book. Sizing is the most underrated skill we teach.
03 — Calendar

The rhythm of the fund.

Rituals beat resolutions. The calendar is what makes the discipline survive midterms.
— Operating Notes · 2026

Twice a month. Pitch meetings on alternate Tuesdays at 6pm. Two pitches per meeting, 15 minutes each, with structured Q&A and a vote at the close.

Quarterly outlooks. Each pod publishes a sector outlook ahead of the calendar quarter. The outlook is presented to the board and informs sizing decisions for the coming three months.

Annual report. The full fund publishes an annual report to the university and the board, summarizing performance, holdings, and key learnings. It is the closest analogue to a real fund's investor letter.

Onboarding and offboarding. September brings the new cohort through structured onboarding — financial modeling, valuation, and pitch mechanics. May is reverse: outgoing PMs document everything for their successors.
